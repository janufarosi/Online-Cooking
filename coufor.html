  • Week 2: Indian Basics (Rice, Dal, Roti)
  • Week 3: North Indian Curries & Street Food
  • Week 4: South Indian Breakfast & Curries

  • Week 6: West Indian Cuisine & Snacks

  • Week 8: Fusion Indian Cooking + Final Project

    • 4.Live Sessions (Zoom/Google Meet for Q&A).

    5.Community Group (WhatsApp, Discord, or Slack for student sharing)

    If using WhatsApp best for small groups, ( 250 students)

  • Create a “Cooking Course Batch [Month-Year]” group.
  • Use broadcast lists for announcements.
  • Students share dish photos, ask quick questions.
  • Best for: casual learners, smaller class sizes.

    • If using Discord (best for large interactive communities)

  • Create a server with multiple channels, for example:
  • #announcements → Instructor posts updates, weekly challenges
  • #recipe-sharing → Students post photos of dishes
  • #doubt-clearing → Questions & answers
  • #regional-food-talk → Cultural discussions
  • #voice-class → Optional live Q&A sessions

    • Engagement Ideas for the Community

  • Weekly Cooking Challenge → e.g., “Best Paneer Dish of the Week”
  • Polls → “Which region’s cuisine should we explore next?”
  • Student Spotlight → Feature one student’s recipe weekly
  • Festive Specials → Onam Sadhya, Diwali Sweets, Eid Biryani challenges
  • Live Quick Tips → Instructor shares 5-min tips (video or text)
